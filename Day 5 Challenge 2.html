How to make Tea

At the very least, tea is a flavourful way of getting enough fluid into your body each day. On top of that, studies have shown teas can help protect your teeth and your heart

Ingredients

  • Tea Bag
  • Water
  • Milk - Dairy/Plant based (Optional)
  • Sugar/Honey (Optional)

Preparation

  1. Select a high-quality loose leaf tea or tea bags depending on your preference. Common options include black tea, green tea, white tea, oolong tea, or herbal tea blends.
  2. Heat fresh, cold water in a kettle. The ideal temperature varies depending on the type of tea:
    • Black tea: Boiling water (100°C/212°F)
    • Green tea: Slightly cooler water (around 80-85°C/176-185°F)
    • White tea: Even cooler water (around 70-80°C/158-176°F)
    • Oolong tea: Hotter water (around 85-90°C/185-194°F)
    • Herbal tea: Boiling water
  3. Pour a small amount of hot water into your teapot or cup to preheat it. This helps maintain the temperature of the brewed tea.
  4. For each cup of tea, use approximately one teaspoon (2-3 grams) of loose leaf tea or one tea bag.
  5. Pour the hot water over the tea leaves or tea bag in your preheated vessel. Let the tea steep for the recommended time:
    • Black tea: 3-5 minutes
    • Green tea: 2-3 minutes
    • White tea: 4-5 minutes
    • Oolong tea: 4-7 minutes
    • Herbal tea: 5-7 minutes
  6. Cover the teapot or cup with a lid or saucer to retain the heat and aroma. Allow the tea to infuse fully during the steeping process.
  7. After the appropriate steeping time, remove the tea leaves or tea bag from the water. If using loose leaf tea, you can use a strainer to catch the leaves as you pour.
  8. Stir well.
  9. Pour the brewed tea into your cup and enjoy! You can drink it as is or add sweeteners, milk, lemon, or other flavourings according to your preference.
